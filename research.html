Current Research Focus

My work at MIT focuses on developing novel computational algorithms for large-scale genomic data analysis, with a specific interest in methods that can handle complex structural variations and data from non-model organisms.

Project 1: Advanced Read Alignment

I am currently developing a new algorithm for rapid, memory-efficient alignment of long sequencing reads, specifically addressing issues related to high error rates and complex genome structures. This involves utilizing machine learning models to improve alignment accuracy and speed.

Project 2: Ancient DNA Analysis Pipeline

In collaboration with the ancient genomics lab, I am contributing to a robust pipeline for quality control and assembly of ancient DNA fragments. Key challenges include high fragmentation and chemical damage patterns, which our pipeline is designed to mitigate.
